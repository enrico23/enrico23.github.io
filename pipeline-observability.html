From Ephemeral Metrics to Durable Signals

Case Study — Delivery Intelligence (CI/CD)

In a previous role, our CI/CD metrics were effective at answering immediate operational questions (“what just broke?”), but insufficient for answering long-horizon ones:

  • Were pipelines getting faster or slower over time?
  • Were changes improving delivery, or shifting bottlenecks?
  • Was additional test coverage still generating proportional confidence?

Context

  • Multi-team environment with high deployment frequency.
  • CI/CD telemetry retained for short windows and optimised for real-time dashboards.
  • Delivery conversations frequently reset once historical context expired.

The system was optimised for reacting to incidents, not for understanding delivery climate over months.

Problem Reframing

The issue was not a lack of dashboards. It was treating delivery metrics as short-lived telemetry rather than durable data.

Without historical continuity, long-term delivery decisions were being made from short-term signals.

Ownership

  • Reframed CI/CD metrics as decision-support data rather than incident-support telemetry.
  • Defined which signals were worth persisting beyond default retention windows.
  • Established a long-term storage and aggregation approach to enable trend analysis.

Implementation Approach

  • Persisted key delivery metrics (pipeline duration, test phase duration, failure rates, deployment frequency).
  • Normalised metrics to allow cross-team comparison over time.
  • Focused on monthly and quarterly trend analysis rather than real-time dashboards.

The goal was not more visualisation. It was stable context.

Tradeoffs

  • Additional upfront effort to define metric semantics clearly.
  • Acceptance that durable analysis would not be instant.
  • Operational ownership of metric integrity over time.

Insights That Emerged

With durable history, patterns became visible that short retention windows had masked.

  • Integration test coverage growth correlated with increasing build times.
  • Time-to-production slowed as full test suites expanded.
  • Failure rate improvements did not scale proportionally with execution cost.

This was not evidence that testing was harmful. It was evidence of diminishing marginal confidence from blanket execution strategies.

Impact

  • Enabled evidence-based discussion about selective test execution strategies.
  • Made throughput vs confidence tradeoffs explicit.
  • Shifted delivery conversations from intuition-driven to data-grounded.
  • Provided longitudinal visibility that influenced ongoing optimisation decisions.

Why It Matters

Short-term metrics help teams react.
Durable metrics help organisations decide.

By treating CI/CD data as durable infrastructure rather than disposable telemetry, we improved the quality of delivery decisions and reduced reliance on anecdotal reasoning.
